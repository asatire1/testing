Americano Format

Americano is the most popular social padel format. Players rotate partners each round, so everyone plays with everyone. Individual points are accumulated across all matches.

How It Works

  • ‚Ä¢ Players rotate partners each round
  • ‚Ä¢ Play matches to a fixed point total (e.g., 32 points)
  • ‚Ä¢ Points split based on score (16-16, 20-12, etc.)
  • ‚Ä¢ Individual leaderboard tracks total points
  • ‚Ä¢ Highest scorer wins

Best For

  • ‚Ä¢ Social club sessions
  • ‚Ä¢ Meeting new players
  • ‚Ä¢ Mixed skill levels
  • ‚Ä¢ 8-16 players
  • ‚Ä¢ 2-4 courts

Example: With 8 players and 2 courts, everyone plays 7 matches (one with each partner). A typical session takes 2-3 hours.

Mexicano Format

Mexicano adds a competitive twist. After the first round, matchups are determined by standings - players with similar points play each other. This creates exciting, closely-matched games throughout.

How It Works

  • ‚Ä¢ Round 1: Random matchups
  • ‚Ä¢ Subsequent rounds: Matched by standings
  • ‚Ä¢ Top players face top players
  • ‚Ä¢ Available in Individual or Team mode
  • ‚Ä¢ Creates competitive balance

Best For

  • ‚Ä¢ Competitive sessions
  • ‚Ä¢ Players wanting close matches
  • ‚Ä¢ 8-24 players
  • ‚Ä¢ Fixed pairs (Team Mexicano)
  • ‚Ä¢ 2-6 courts

Individual vs Team Mexicano

Individual: Partners rotate (like Americano) but matchups are by standings.
Team: Fixed pairs compete, teams matched by standings.

Team Tournament Format

Team Tournament is a traditional league format with fixed pairs. Teams are split into two groups, play round-robin within their group, then the top teams advance to knockout playoffs.

How It Works

  • ‚Ä¢ Fixed pairs (teams of 2)
  • ‚Ä¢ Two groups (Group A & B)
  • ‚Ä¢ Round-robin within groups
  • ‚Ä¢ Top teams advance to knockouts
  • ‚Ä¢ Semi-finals and Final

Best For

  • ‚Ä¢ Established partnerships
  • ‚Ä¢ Club championships
  • ‚Ä¢ Competitive events
  • ‚Ä¢ 4-8 teams (8-16 players)

Mix Tournament Format

Mix Tournament is designed for larger groups (20-28 players). Players are split into groups of 4, play round-robin within their group, then the top performers advance to knockout rounds.

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

Is this app really free?

Yes, completely free! No registration, no hidden costs, no limits on tournaments. We built this for the padel community.

Do players need to create accounts?

No. The organiser creates the tournament and shares a link. Players just open the link to view scores - no signup required.

Can multiple people enter scores?

Only the organiser (with the organiser link) can edit scores. This prevents accidental changes. Players can view everything but not edit.

How long do tournaments stay available?

Tournaments are stored indefinitely. You can return to your tournament link anytime to view results.

What devices work with this app?

Any device with a web browser - phones, tablets, laptops, desktops. Works on iPhone, Android, Windows, Mac, everything.

Can I use this for my padel club?

Absolutely! Many clubs use Uber Padel for their weekly social sessions and tournaments. It's designed for exactly this.

What's the difference between Americano and Mexicano?

In Americano, matchups are pre-set and everyone plays with everyone. In Mexicano, matchups are dynamic based on standings, so you play against people with similar scores.
